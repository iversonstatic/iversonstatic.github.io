Floralversal

This project involved creating a logo, brand, and packaging for a up-and-coming skincare line called Floralversal. Instead of high-end stores, Floralversal’s products were to be sold at affordable prices at common drug stores. The company wanted their branding to reflect the ‘universal’ and ‘versatility’ of their lotions, while also drawing attention to the fragrances used.

DELIVERABLES: Logo and Brand Design, Packaging Design
KEY SKILLS USED: Brand Design, Typography
TOOLS USED: Adobe Illustrator, Adobe Photoshop, Adobe InDesign
Branding & Logo Design

To counter the competition, the branding had to be much more extravagant compared to the cold and medical appearance of their drugstore counterparts. Through typographic modification, I was able to encorporate a flower into the lettering and also create a symbol pattern for packaging.

Packaging Design

For packaging design, I wanted to find a balance between maximalism and simplicity. Using floral imagery with three-dimensional textures helps add visual flavor to the frame of the bottles, while never detracting from the information. The simplicity comes into play with the body typography, plainly listing product facts about the recommended skin type and ingredients in the center.

Advertising

Two types of advertisements were designed, one focusing on print magazine marketing and the other on mobile advertising. These ads played on reaching targetted audiences with specific skin textures, focusing again on the statement that "skin care is universal". The mobile advertisement also had a call to action included to help increase the click through ratio.
